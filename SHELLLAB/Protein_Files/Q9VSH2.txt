CC   -!- FUNCTION: Gustatory receptor required for response to the bitter in
CC       taste neurons. Gr66a cells respond to bitter compounds such as
CC       caffeine, theophylline, threonine or valine. Flies avoid bitter
CC       substances, suggesting that Gr66a neuron activity is sufficient to
CC       mediate avoidance behavior. Required for sensing and avoiding N,N-
CC       Diethyl-meta-toluamide (DEET), the most widely used insect repellent
CC       worldwide, as well as to L-canavanine, a plant-derived insecticide.
CC       Gr66a neurons are also involved in the sex-specific perception of
CC       molecules inducing male avoidance behavior, probably through sensing 7-
CC       tricosene (7-T), a male cuticular pheromone and leading to inhibition
CC       of male-male courtship. Finally, also plays a role in oviposition
CC       behavior, in which females evaluate their environment and choose to lay
CC       eggs on substrates they may find aversive in other contexts.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15202999, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16423701,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16979558, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19246397,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19618260, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20797533,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22279227, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22798487}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Taste hairs in labial palps, labral and cibarial
CC       sense organs and forelegs. In larvae, is expressed in neurons of the
CC       terminal external chemosensory organ, as well as in the dorsal,
CC       ventral, and posterior pharyngeal sense organs.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11516643, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15202999,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16423701, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16979558,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22031876}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Disrupts fly aversion to caffeine, N,N-Diethyl-
CC       meta-toluamide (DEET), and L-canavanine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16979558,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19246397, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20797533,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22279227}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the insect chemoreceptor superfamily. Gustatory
CC       receptor (GR) family. Gr66a subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
