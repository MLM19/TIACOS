CC   -!- FUNCTION: Gustatory receptor which mediates acceptance or avoidance
CC       behavior, depending on its substrates. Required for sensing all
CC       nonvolatile repulsive chemicals, including tastants, pheromones, and
CC       especially N,N-Diethyl-meta-toluamide (DEET), the most widely used
CC       insect repellent worldwide. Functions also as a pheromone receptor for
CC       a male inhibitory pheromone leading to male-male courtship suppression.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19765987, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20797533}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed widely in gustatory receptor neurons
CC       (GRNs) that respond to aversive chemicals. In larvae, is expressed in
CC       neurons of the terminal external chemosensory organ, and the dorsal,
CC       ventral and posterior external chemosensory organs.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19765987, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22031876}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Impairs avoiding all nonvolatile repellents
CC       tested, ranging from quinine to denatonium, lobeline, caffeine, and
CC       N,N-Diethyl-meta-toluamide (DEET). Also displays increased male-to-male
CC       courtship. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19765987, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20797533}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the insect chemoreceptor superfamily. Gustatory
CC       receptor (GR) family. Gr66a subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
